Common Scam Texts in Images

Urgent Action Required

Claims of account problems, limited-time offers, or urgent requests that pressure you to act quickly without thinking.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers

Promises of free money, prizes, lottery winnings, or unrealistic investment returns with little or no effort.

Requests for Personal Information

Phishing attempts claiming to be from banks, government agencies, or known companies asking for passwords, account details, or personal data.

QR Code Scams

Images with QR codes that lead to malicious websites, fake payment portals, or malware downloads.

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Verify the sender through official channels before responding to any request
  • Never click links or scan QR codes from untrusted sources
  • Don't share personal or financial information in response to unsolicited messages
  • Check for poor grammar, misspellings, and generic greetings - these are common in scam texts
  • Remember that legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive information through images
