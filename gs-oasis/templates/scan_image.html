How Our Image Scanner Works

Multi-Layer Detection

Our advanced image scanner analyzes your image through multiple security layers:

  • Visual Analysis: Checks for manipulated images, unusual dimensions, and suspicious visual patterns
  • Text Extraction: Uses OCR technology to find and analyze all text in the image
  • Hidden Content Detection: Searches for concealed information like steganography or metadata
  • QR Code Scanning: Identifies QR codes and examines embedded URLs for threats

What We Look For

Our system identifies these common risk indicators:

  • Urgent Language: Terms like "act now," "limited time," or "urgent action required"
  • Financial Bait: Promises of free money, prizes, lottery winnings, or unrealistic returns
  • Personal Information Requests: Attempts to collect sensitive data like passwords or account details
  • Suspicious URLs: Links to unfamiliar or deceptive websites
  • Image Manipulation: Signs of editing, unusual compression, or hidden data
  • Technical Deception: Claims about viruses, hacking, or technical support needs

Risk Assessment

After analyzing your image, we provide:

  • Threat Detection: Identification of potential scams or malicious content
  • Confidence Level: Percentage indicating how certain we are about the analysis
  • Specific Warnings: Detailed explanation of detected issues
  • Safety Recommendations: Guidance on how to proceed based on results

Text Analysis in Images

OCR Text Extraction

Our system uses Optical Character Recognition (OCR) to extract and analyze all visible text in your images, identifying potential scam content.

Urgent Action Required

Claims of account problems, limited-time offers, or urgent requests that pressure you to act quickly without thinking.

Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers

Promises of free money, prizes, lottery winnings, or unrealistic investment returns with little or no effort.

Requests for Personal Information

Phishing attempts claiming to be from banks, government agencies, or known companies asking for passwords, account details, or personal data.

QR Code Scams

Images with QR codes that lead to malicious websites, fake payment portals, or malware downloads.

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Our scanner automatically extracts and analyzes all text in the image
  • Verify the sender through official channels before responding to any request
  • Never click links or scan QR codes from untrusted sources
  • Don't share personal or financial information in response to unsolicited messages
  • Check for poor grammar, misspellings, and generic greetings - these are common in scam texts
  • Remember that legitimate organizations won't ask for sensitive information through images
