Phishing Scams

What are Phishing Scams?

Phishing is a type of online scam where criminals send an email or create a website that appears to be from a legitimate company, asking for sensitive information such as passwords, credit card numbers, or personal details.

Common Signs of Phishing

  • Emails or messages with poor spelling and grammar
  • Requests for personal information
  • Urgent or threatening language
  • Strange or mismatched URLs
  • Suspicious attachments

How to Protect Yourself

  • Never click on suspicious links in emails
  • Check the sender's email address carefully
  • Don't provide personal information in response to emails
  • Use our link scanner to verify suspicious URLs
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts

Example of a Phishing Email

From: security@bankk-verification.com

Subject: URGENT: Your Account Has Been Compromised

Dear Valued Customer,

We have detected suspicious activity on your account. To prevent unauthorized access, please verify your account information immediately by clicking the link below:

https://secure.bank-verification.com/verify

Failure to verify within 24 hours will result in your account being suspended.

Red Flags: Urgent language, suspicious sender email, request for personal information, slightly misspelled domain name

Malware & Viruses

What is Malware?

Malware refers to any software specifically designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system. Common types include viruses, trojans, worms, ransomware, and spyware.

Common Signs of Malware Infection

  • Computer running slower than usual
  • Unexpected pop-up windows
  • Programs starting or closing automatically
  • Unusual hard drive activity
  • Missing files or changed settings

How to Protect Yourself

  • Keep your operating system and software updated
  • Use reputable antivirus software
  • Be cautious about what you download and install
  • Use our file scanner to check suspicious files
  • Backup your important data regularly

Example of Malware Distribution

Scenario: Free Software Download Trap

You search for "free PDF converter" and click on a result that offers free software. The website has multiple download buttons, many of which are actually ads. When you click what appears to be the download button, you receive a file named "PDFConverter_Setup.exe" but it contains hidden malware that installs alongside the legitimate software.

Red Flags: Too many download buttons, no clear publisher information, file name doesn't match the official software name
